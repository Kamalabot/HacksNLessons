Passion:

I am from a same background as you are. In addition, I am a mechanical engineer. I love working with computers, data, automating data visualisation and data acquisition so I started learning to code. It is all about the idea of helping others to solve the problem.

Speed & Automation :

Many like me have shifted to DS field, only to face the culture shock. The shock will be the speed at which one has to complete the code, test, error correct, test and deploy. Some companies have DevOps, automating this process. But in some, you may have to set it up using a vendor. And in some, you may have to code the entire automation pipeline, since they hired you… For that, you have to learn, perform and in parallel deliver…. The speed and automation aspect!!!

Algorithms:

Another aspect is the algorithms. DS is about problem solving better than your competitor. As a Data Scientist, our task will be to come up with algorithm that is better than competitor. On top of it, algorithm needs to be implemented efficiently to run on Data Pipeline. Algorithms have to execute in seconds, because the customers will move away if your solution is slow.

Mindset:

You will be surprised how many Mechanical Engineering best practices are used by software dev guys, but the speed with which they do it is mind blowing. When we enter Coding field from manufacturing, our mindset has to be tuned for the different way of working. Don’t underestimate the time it will take to change your own mindset… It will take more time than we estimate.

Real Projects:

Many times the companies ask for the real project experience during the interview process. There are many sites, services that can help you creating the projects. They are still toy projects at the best. The real software development involves lot more than just creating the models, and needs many different team members. It is same like in manufacturing. Any one can make a shaft using a lathe, but there has to be a quality engineer doing the testing for lot after lot, and providing the report. When you learn coding, you can think of new ideas to bring the manufacturing the data science world together. That is where starting your own business will come into picture.
